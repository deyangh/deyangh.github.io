Spatio-Temporal Wildfire Prediction

Research Assistant | FORTIS Lab, USC | January 2025 - Present

Advisor: Professor Zhao Yue

Developing robust, trustworthy, and scalable AI systems for wildfire prediction, emphasizing anomaly detection and out-of-distribution generalization across diverse environmental conditions.

Key Contributions:

  • Temporal Fusion Transformer (TFT) Architecture: Designed and implemented an interpretable transformer-based model combining LSTM for temporal patterns and multi-head self-attention for long-range dependencies, achieving F1 scores of 0.24-0.28 in wildfire prediction tasks.
  • Graph Neural Networks for Spatial Modeling: Integrated Graph Convolutional Networks (GCN) to capture spatial dependencies across county adjacency networks, improving prediction accuracy by leveraging geographic correlations.
  • Multi-Source Data Fusion: Architected data pipelines processing ERA5 weather reanalysis (temperature, wind, precipitation, solar radiation) and vegetation indices (LAI, NDVI) across 116,000+ time-county observations spanning 2021-2025.
  • Production-Scale Optimization: Implemented A100 GPU optimizations (TF32 acceleration, torch.compile, mixed precision training) achieving 10-50x speedup over CPU baselines, reducing full training time from 8+ hours to 25-35 minutes.
  • Advanced Loss Functions: Developed Focal Loss implementation with adaptive thresholding to address severe class imbalance (8.2:1 negative-to-positive ratio), improving model robustness for rare fire events.
  • Model Evaluation & Deployment: Designed comprehensive evaluation pipelines with automated assessment processes, generating geospatial predictions and training analytics for production deployment.
